How to Identify Safe Food to Eat

Chop the plant into different parts (roots, stems, leaves, buds, and flowers) and the check to see if it has a strong or unpleasant odour. Next, test whether the plant has any contact poison by rubbing the plant against your skin to see if it has any reactions. If it passes the skin test, try boiling it before you eat it. Before you take a bite, make sure to press some of the plant to your mouth to see if it burns or itches and wait for 15 minutes.
